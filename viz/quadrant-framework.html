Four contexts, four pathways

The water-food nexus framework groups countries by freshwater stress and net food trade position. Each context implies a different starting point for agricultural water management.

Low freshwater stress High freshwater stress
Net food importers Net food exporters
1

Water-secure food importers

Water surplus, calorie deficit

An unprecedented opportunity

  • Expand irrigation and AWM responsibly to reduce import dependence
  • Use trade strategically where agroclimate or market access dictate
  • Internalize risks of sourcing from water-stressed countries
4

Water-stressed food importers

Water deficit, calorie deficit

Precarious dependence, structural change

  • Shift to high-value crops with limited water (economic productivity)
  • Diversify the economy and protect transitions with safety nets
  • Augment supply with reused treated wastewater
  • Enforce water use caps; diversify import sources
2

Water-secure food exporters

Water surplus, calorie surplus

An opportunity for global food security

  • Strategically and responsibly expand exports
  • Pair AWM expansion with local and global value chains
  • Help rebalance the global water-food equation
3

Water-stressed food exporters

Water deficit, calorie surplus

An unsustainable contradiction

  • Shift to crops with lower water use per unit return
  • Modernize irrigation; incentivize sustainable practices
  • Augment supply with reused treated wastewater
  • Buffer transitions with financial, technical, policy support
